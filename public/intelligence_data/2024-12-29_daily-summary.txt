Daily Overview
The US housing market is experiencing a historic affordability crisis, with potential price corrections looming due to multiple converging factors. High mortgage rates, declining institutional investment, and changing migration patterns are creating significant challenges for the real estate market, suggesting a potential downturn in home prices and market dynamics.

Macro
- Housing affordability is at its second-worst level since the 1970s, potentially at the worst all-in cost level ever
- Existing home sales are at their lowest point since 1995, with over 50% of homeowners unable to afford homes at current mortgage rates
- Institutional investors are becoming net sellers, particularly in Sun Belt and Mountain West markets
- Short-term rental markets are experiencing revenue declines for 14-16 months
- Home builders are cutting prices and offering incentives to maintain sales volumes
- Inventory is rising across most US markets, especially in Sun Belt and Mountain West regions
- Potential economic risks include rising unemployment and increasing mortgage delinquencies
- Labor market remains a key stabilizing factor for housing prices

Overarching Themes and Trends
- Housing market facing significant affordability challenges
- Structural shifts in real estate investment and ownership patterns
- Increasing market volatility in regional housing markets
- Potential for broader economic impact due to real estate market corrections
- Importance of monitoring labor market stability as a key economic indicator
