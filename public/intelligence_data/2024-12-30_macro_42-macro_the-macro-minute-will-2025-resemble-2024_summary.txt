Key Insight
The global macro landscape for 2025 is expected to be significantly different from 2024, with potential challenges including substantial global debt refinancing needs, policy changes, and economic uncertainties that could impact asset markets.

<list_of_important_information>
- Global liquidity cycle analysis indicates 2025 will feature one of the most significant global refinancing needs ever across private and public sector debt
- Potential positive changes for asset markets include:
  - Tax cuts
  - Increased mergers and acquisitions (M&A)

- Potential negative changes for asset markets include:
  - Potential implementation of tariffs
  - Risk of a too strong US dollar
  - Potential stagflation
  - Lowering of the Federal Reserve's "put" (economic support mechanism)

- Potential economic policy changes under a potential Trump administration:
  - Dramatic spending cuts
  - Aggressive tariff implementation
  - Potential changes in net financing policy
  - Tighter border policy to slow labor market growth

- Deficit reduction target: Reduce deficit-to-GDP ratio from current 7.1% to 4%

- Key economic strategy discussed: Potentially "kitchen sinking" the economy early in an administration to create conditions for future recovery
